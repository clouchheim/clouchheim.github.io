About Me

Hi, I’m Carter Louchheim, a computer science major at Williams College with a passion for blending technology, linguistics, and the outdoors into meaningful projects. Growing up in the mountains of Utah, I developed a deep love for skiing and the outdoors, competing as an alpine ski racer while learning the value of discipline, perseverance, and teamwork. This connection to the natural world fuels my drive to create tech-driven solutions that intersect with real-world challenges, such as predicting avalanche conditions or analyzing sports performance data. Academically and professionally, I specialize in software engineering, data science, and natural language processing (NLP), with a particular interest in low-resource languages. My recent thesis project focuses on building neural machine translation systems to preserve and translate low-resrouce languages. Whether I’m coding an ML model, designing an intuitive data visualization tool, or collaborating with stakeholders, I aim to bring curiosity, creativity, and problem-solving to everything I do. Outside of my technical work, you’ll find me exploring the mountains, or seeking new outdoor adventures. I believe the combination of a love for nature and a passion for technology can lead to solutions that are not only innovative but also impactful.

Let’s collaborate and bring your vision to life!

Accessibility Audit: TikTok and Physical/Motor Disabilities

This project involved conducting an accessibility audit of TikTok for users with motor and physical disabilities, utilizing frameworks from Design Justice, first-person testimonies, and accessibility standards. The audit identified barriers such as small touch targets, lack of keyboard navigation, and time-sensitive interactions that hindered usability for users with limited dexterity. Recommendations included implementing customizable interface elements, enhancing keyboard accessibility, and involving disabled users in the design process to create a more inclusive platform.

Summarizer Browser Extension

In this project, my team developed a Chrome browser extension that summarizes highlighted text on web pages using a LLM API. We conducted iterative prototyping and user testing to refine features, including customizable summary formats, improved accuracy, and an option to replace original text with the summary while maintaining a clean, minimal interface. User feedback guided key design improvements such as adding usage instructions, enhancing visual design, and balancing feature richness with simplicity to improve overall accessibility and usability.

Designing for Interactivity: JewelJet and Hand Gesture Control

This project explored using hand gestures as an alternative control method to improve accessibility in the web game Jewel Jet by leveraging the MediaPipe Gesture Recognizer library. After testing multiple games, Jewel Jet was chosen for its simple mechanics that suited gesture-based input, and my team designed intuitive gestures mapped to game actions. The project demonstrated that gesture controls can enhance gameplay accessibility and provide a more immersive experience without disrupting game flow.

Needfinding for WHOOP

This analysis examines WHOOP's screenless design and its impact on user interaction, emphasizing the cognitive demands of interpreting complex health metrics through the mobile app. Interviews with three athletes reveal varied usage patterns, highlighting the device's role in long-term training optimization and the importance of mental skill over physical interaction. The study underscores the significance of user-centered design in wearable technology, particularly in balancing data complexity with accessibility.
